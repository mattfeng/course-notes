# Lecture 7. Conformational Isomers 2: Cyclohexane, Chairs; Structure and Energy
- Energy barrier (<C chem="\Delta G^{\ddagger}" />) determines the rate of interconversion (**kinetics!**)
- Common values for energy barriers
  - Ring flip (chair flip): <C chem="\pu{10.8 kcal/mol}" />
  - Bond rotation in ethane: <C chem="\pu{3.7 kcal/mol}" />
  - Inversion of simple amines: <C chem="\pu{5 kcal/mol}" />
- <C chem="\pu{17.4 kcal/mol}" /> is one flip per second
- Slow interconversion: <C chem="\pu{24 kcal/mol}" />
  - Two factors: **small rings** and **electronegative substituents**
  - Small rings mean transition state has high angle strain

# Lecture 8. Structure and Energy (Continued)
- Relation between free energy change of reaction and equilibrium constant: <C chem="\Delta G^\circ = -RT \ln \Keq" />
  - <C chem="\Delta G^\circ = \pu{-1.36 kcal/mol} \implies \Keq = 10" />
- Gibbs free energy: <C chem="\DG = \DH - T\DS" />
- **Bond dissociation energy/enthalpy (BDE).** enthalpy approximates free energy; the energy required to break a bond homolytically.
- BDE $\uparrow$ $=$ bond length $\downarrow$
- Use the <C chem="\pu{1.36 kcal/mol}" /> to **quantitatively compare bond strengths** (e.g. <C chem="\pu{3 kcal/mol}" /> means bond is 100 times more stable)

## Evaluating the energy of organic molecules
1. **Step 1.** Evaluate $\Delta H^\circ$
2. **Step 2.** Consider correction factors
    - **torsional strain.** destabilizes molecule
    - **angle strain.** destabilizes molecule
    - **steric strain.** destabilizes molecule
    - **$\pi$-electron delocalization.** stabilizes molecule
