## archambault, _one beer, one block_ (2018)
- meanings of cement
    - aspiration
    - social mobility
- cell phones were the fashion; now cement is the fashion
- cement is so important that it competes with buying beer
- inhambane, mozambique
- if you have cement, you should make blocks; otherwise
    - it can blow away
    - it gets stolen (siphoned away)
- moving up in life
    - first build a reed house
    - then accumulate blocks
    - then build a concrete house
- seeing the amount of the blocks _boosts morale_ (**momentum**)
    - reminded of your goals; a **mnemonic device**
- cement doesn't just _represent_ aspiration; it _enables_ aspiration.
- **social reproduction.** the processes that ensure the self-perpetuation of a social structure over time
    - certain things are required in order for people to move out, get a job, etc. and reproduce social structures
- start with the broader story (crisis of social reproduction), and discuss how concrete enables it
