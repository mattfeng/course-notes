## key concepts

### specific heat

- for perfect gases, $\bar{C}_v = 3/2R$
- for perfect gases, $\bar{C}_p - \bar{C}_v = R$

### adiabatic processes
- for perfect gases under reversible processes
    - $\frac{T_2}{T_1} = \left(\frac{V_1}{V_2}\right)^R$
    - $P_1V_1^\gamma = P_2V_2^\gamma$, where $\gamma = \frac{\bar{C}_p}{\bar{C}_v}$

### kirchoff's law of thermochemistry
- assuming $\Delta C_p$ is constant (it wouldn't be constant if $C_{p,\text{prod}}$ changes faster or slower than $C_{p,\text{reactants}}$), $\Delta_{T_2} H = \Delta_{T_1} H + \Delta C_p \Delta T$
- [Wikipedia](https://archive.is/gwMui)

### entropy
- **standard entropy** ($S^\circ$) is the absolute entropy of a substance
    - atm: <C chem="\pu{1 atm}" />
    - temp: <C chem="\pu{298.15 K}" />
- **entropy version of kirchoff's law.** assuming $\Delta C_p$ is constant, $\Delta_{T_2}S = \Delta_{T_1} S + \Delta C_p \ln \left(\frac{T_2}{T_1}\right)$
